## What I learned from 6 years of building CodeNewbie with Saron Yitbarek

Saron is the founder of CodeNewbie and she talks about her experience building CodeNewbie.

Acquired by DEV 6 months ago, it's podcasts and meetups.

Goes over her coding journey in non-tech roles. Noticed engineers were the most valued in the company - she was interested in developing her skills in this field.

Tok some time to code on her own , then joined the FlatIron bootcamp. So expensive to experience this community when struggling, she wanted to crete her own that's free.

Started on Twitter to meet other coders and discuss what they are doing.

1. People need to feel safe and loved: There is a place for you in the tech industry.
2. It's not about a place, it's about a shared experience.
3. Rules recruit and filter: Being supportive and kind are required.
4. People are generally helpful, but it's up to you to ask for help.
5. The power of Twitter mentorship: Reach out to hel p others who are looking for a job, to better their skills, etc.
6. Stories are everything: Started CodeNewbie podcast for topics too big for Twitter - to focus on CodeNewbie journey, get to know the people behind the code.
7. Representation matters: Career tracks, backgrounds, don't have CS degrees.
8. To get out of the way: Saron pointed out how she rarely speaks in her podcast episodes in order to center the guest she's speaking with.
9. There's no one way to do it: No one way to become a developer.
10. Junior developers are assets: They force you to know what you are talking about, have good documentation, an collaborate and mentor others, have a lot to offer. I wish companies valued junior developers more that I see.
11. Consistency is powerful: 100 Days of Code Campaign to push someone to get into the habit of coding and making progress.
12. To capture the value you create.
13. Sustainability if important: Pushed Saron to make CodeNewbie full-time.
14. Accessibility is everywhere: Blind listener appreciated learning from a podcast (baseCS) since most learning resources are visual based.
15. Feedback is love: Someone trusts that you care enough to do this thing, these thoughts.
16. Small, consistent acts build trust.
17. Application > tooling: It's important to know what the tech is being sed for, rather than how it's make.
18. Everyone's a newbie: We're always learning something new to challenge us.

Saron also spoke to us about her mental health journey while working on CodeNewbie.

19. Drugs are great.
20. Mental health is self care.

## You and Me Learn All About HTTP with Safia Abdalla

Safia goes over what HTTP is and the different web protocols that exist.

HTTP stands for Hyper Text Transfer Protocol.

HyperText - hyperlinks (links to tother images, etc on the web) with text.

Transfer - protocol relates in a way hypertext is exchanged between devices

Interactive option to follow along in dev tools to learn about HTTP exploration.

Use Network tab to see outgoing requests from the web browser or native app and responses from the server.

GET, POST - post info to web server, PUT, DELETE

different headers: type of resource transferred

HTTP pipelining is a feature where multiple requests can be sent at the same time to and their res can be processes separately instead of waiting for each to succeed one by one.

HTTPS secures transmission of hypertext documents.

Safia also introduces the network stack how all the protocols relate to the others.

HTTPs is TLS (how messages should be encrypted and using authentication - security) applied to HTTP (for transmitting messages) using TCP (Transmission Control Protocol).

View Safia's talk [here](https://dev.to/captainsafia/you-and-me-learn-all-about-http-with-safia-abdalla-3nd0).
